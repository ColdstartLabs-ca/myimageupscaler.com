## What Drives CTR in SERPs

Before the fixes, here are the levers Google Search users respond to:

1. **Year** — `[2026]` signals freshness. Posts on time-sensitive topics without it lose clicks to fresher-looking results.
2. **Numbers** — `Top 10`, `12 Best`, `3 Steps` create a concrete promise.
3. **Power words** — `Free`, `Tested`, `No Signup`, `No Watermark`, `No Photoshop` lower perceived friction.
4. **CTA in description** — `Try free`, `Upload now`, `No download needed` — active verbs that invite a click.
5. **Differentiated SEO title** — The page `<h1>` and the SERP title serve different jobs. The h1 orients readers; the SERP title sells the click.
6. **Differentiated meta description** — Same logic. The SERP snippet is ad copy, not a chapter intro.

## Measurement

After edits, track in GSC (Google Search Console):

- **Impressions** — should stay stable or rise (titles still match intent)
- **CTR** — target +15–30% on edited posts within 4–8 weeks
- **Average position** — may shift slightly; acceptable if CTR improves

Use the `gsc-analysis` skill to pull per-page CTR data before and after as a baseline.

## Notes

- `sample-article-title-for-testing` — verify this is not a live indexed page before editing or delete if it's a test artifact.
- Do NOT change page `<h1>` titles, only `seo_title` and `seo_description` fields. The h1 is for readers; the SEO fields are for SERP.
- Keep descriptions under 160 characters to avoid truncation.
